Pan-Seared Salmon with Citrus Gremolata

schedule 25 Mins
local_fire_department 420 Cal
link Instagram: @chefstable

Ingredients

Adjust Servings 2 People
  • Fresh Atlantic Salmon Fillets
    2 (6oz)
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
    2 tbsp
  • Fresh Italian Parsley
    1/2 cup
  • Lemon Zest & Juice
    1 unit
  • Garlic Cloves, minced
    2 units
  • Kosher Salt & Pepper
    to taste
lightbulb

Chef's Note For the crispiest skin, pat the salmon dry with paper towels before seasoning. Don't move it once it hits the pan!

Instructions

1

Prepare the Salmon

Remove the salmon fillets from the refrigerator 15 minutes before cooking. Pat them extremely dry on all sides using paper towels. Season generously with kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper.

2

Sear the Fillets

Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed stainless steel or cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, place fillets skin-side down. Press gently with a spatula for 10 seconds.

Active Timer 05:00
3

Make the Gremolata

While the salmon sears, finely chop the parsley. In a small bowl, combine parsley, lemon zest, minced garlic, and a splash of olive oil. Mix well to create a bright, chunky herb paste.

4

Flip and Finish

Carefully flip the fillets. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the salmon is just cooked through but still moist in the center. Top immediately with the gremolata and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
